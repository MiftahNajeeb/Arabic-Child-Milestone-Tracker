# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Arabic-speaking parents in Libya and across the Arab world who want a clearer way to understand their child's early development, especially when they are worried about possible delays and need guidance they can trust. Secondary audiences include judges, partners, mentors, and public-interest collaborators evaluating whether the project is credible, needed, and worth supporting.

## Product Purpose
Tifl exists to close a real gap: there is no accurate, Arabic-first equivalent to the CDC's child milestone tracker for families in the Arab world. The landing page should explain that gap quickly, show why the project matters, and make the app feel trustworthy, humane, and ready to grow into a serious public-interest product.

## Brand Personality
Hopeful, human, modern. The voice should feel calm and supportive rather than clinical or alarmist. It should communicate care, credibility, and practical usefulness for parents while still feeling polished enough for pitches, reviews, and public presentation.

## Anti-references
Do not make this feel like a paid SaaS startup, growth product, or generic AI-generated landing page. Avoid startup-template gradients, glassmorphism, vanity metrics, empty corporate polish, and language that implies monetization. Avoid sterile hospital UI, cartoonish kids-app energy, and luxury-editorial styling that would distract from the project's public-service mission.

## Design Principles
1. Lead with trust before novelty.
2. Make Arabic-first credibility visible, not just stated.
3. Explain the gap clearly, then show a humane path forward.
4. Keep the interface pitch-ready without feeling commercial.
5. Favor reassuring clarity over visual noise or urgency theater.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard accessibility across the landing page. Respect reduced-motion preferences. Treat Arabic-first communication and RTL sensitivity as core inclusion requirements, even when the page includes English copy for broader audiences. Prefer clear structure, readable type, and explicit affordances over decorative effects.
