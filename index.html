Arabic Child Milestone Tracker

Arabic Child Milestone Tracker is a free, Arabic-first mobile application that helps parents track their child's early development and recognize potential delays at the stage when intervention matters most.

The project is built on a foundation of medical credibility. Its developmental milestones are modeled on the framework of the United States CDC Milestone Tracker, bringing that same standard of expert-reviewed accuracy into Arabic for families in Libya and across the Arab world.

Why this matters

Medical credibility, translated into Arabic for the first time.

The aim is a tool parents can trust because it is grounded in established medical standards, and one doctors can rely on because it strengthens the conversation between families and clinicians rather than replacing it.

In the United States, the CDC Milestone Tracker is trusted by millions of families and recommended by doctors because every milestone in it was reviewed and approved by pediatric and child-development experts.

Arabic Child Milestone Tracker is designed to bring that same standard of accuracy into Arabic, adapting it culturally and linguistically for families in Libya and across the Arab world.

What makes the project valuable is the gap it fills. A reliable, clinically grounded way to monitor child development in Arabic simply does not exist today, even though the need spans more than 400 million Arabic speakers.

Why this matters now

When concerns appear early, families need something clearer than guesswork and more responsible than informal advice.

What changes in practice

Parents get a structured, understandable way to follow milestones, watch for warning signs, and arrive at appointments better prepared.

What the product is not

It is not a diagnosis engine. It is a trustworthy support tool that helps parents notice, document, and discuss development earlier.
